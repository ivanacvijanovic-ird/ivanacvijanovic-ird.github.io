Why 1.5 °C ?

Home About Publications CV Contact

It is beyond essential that we stabilize the global warming below 1.5 °C above the preindustrial levels.

Achieving this target, also advocated for in the Paris Agreement, is the only way to avoid huge loss of life and biodiversity on our planet and not risk exceeding multiple tipping points.

Differences between 1.5 °C and 2 °C worlds are immense. See this interactive summary by Carbon Brief.

We are currently on the pathway of exceeding both of these limits, with recent policies & action estimated to result in 2.6 °C global warming (November 2025). Note that the world politics took couple of sharp turns since.
